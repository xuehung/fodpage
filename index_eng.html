Food Open Data

Food Open Data aims to collect product data of packaged foods, including nutrition labels, ingredients and manufacturer information. By creating such a food database, consumers may learn how to eat smarter, and food manufacturers, develop healthier foods to satisfy the market demand.

Why

We eat packaged foods almost everyday. But we are not in control of what we eat. Nor are the food manufacturers.

Consumer

True, there is a product label on the bottle of drinks, the bag of noodles, and the bottle of cooking oils. But do we understand what the label says? Is the food safe? Is it healthy? How does it compare to other foods? Or how does it compare to the same food of other brand?

Food Brands and Channels

Most food brands produce foods that their competitors produced. Is there a way to change the game? How can we detect the change of consumer appetites and become a market leader? How can we use the nutrition data of our food products to improve the returns on investment in brand marketing? How do we use data to engage our consumers? How do we create a "healthy" brand?
